Laing

Day 2 of 30: A creamy and spicy Filipino dish made from dried taro leaves slowly simmered in coconut milk with chilies and aromatics.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried taro leaves
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup coconut cream (kakang gata)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 3-5 red chilies (siling labuyo), whole or sliced
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 2 tbsp dried shrimp (hibe) or fish sauce

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer. Do not boil to avoid curdling.
  3. Carefully add dried taro leaves without stirring. Let them soak and soften in the coconut milk.
  4. Once softened, gently mix the leaves into the sauce.
  5. Add chilies and optional dried shrimp or fish sauce if using. Simmer uncovered until the liquid is mostly absorbed.
  6. Pour in the coconut cream and simmer until thick and creamy.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot with steamed rice.
